EmoInstruct-TTS: Dual-Path Instruction-Guided Emotional Speech Synthesis

Abstract: Instruction-based controllable speech synthesis offers a flexible interface for specifying speaking styles and emotions through natural language. However, existing approaches often rely on coarse emotion labels and lack explicit mechanisms to model fine-grained emotion intensity and variability. In this paper, we propose EmoInstruct-TTS, a dual-path instruction-guided framework for fine-grained emotional speech synthesis. At the representation level, we introduce Emotion2embed, a semantic--acoustic emotion embedding trained under explicit supervision over 48 emotional states, including 27 Fine-Grained Emotion Categories and 21 emotion--intensity combinations. Under controlled conditions, Emotion2embed exhibits directionally ordered variations across discrete intensity levels, enabling structured modeling of graded emotional strength. To infer emotion embeddings from free-form instructions, we propose an Instruction-Conditioned Emotion Flow Model (ICE-Flow), which learns to generate instruction-conditioned embeddings using acoustically grounded emotion representations as supervision targets. The inferred emotion embeddings are integrated into an LLM-based synthesis pipeline to provide explicit and controllable emotional guidance, while semantic planning is handled by the language model. Experimental results show that EmoInstruct-TTS improves emotional controllability and speech naturalness over strong baselines, particularly in modeling fine-grained emotion intensity variations.
